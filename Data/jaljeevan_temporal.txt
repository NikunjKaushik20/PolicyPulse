Year 2019:
Jal Jeevan Mission was launched on August 15, 2019 with the ambitious goal of providing tap water connections to every rural household by 2024. At launch, only 3.23 crore households (17%) had tap water while 15.8 crore households needed connections. The mission received initial allocation of Rs 3.6 lakh crore to be spent over 5 years. Focus was on source sustainability, greywater management, and water quality. States were given flexibility in implementation with 50:50 cost sharing.

Year 2020:
Despite COVID disruptions, 2.8 crore new connections were provided reaching 6 crore total households. The mission adapted with contactless implementation protocols and local labor preference. Pandemic highlighted importance of household water access for handwashing and hygiene. Budget allocation increased to Rs 23,000 crore for the year. Several states achieved significant progress including Goa and Telangana.

Year 2021:
Mission achieved 8 crore household coverage reaching 42% rural households. Har Ghar Jal certification awarded to 67 districts and over 1 lakh villages. Focus intensified on quality through network of water testing laboratories. Grey water management integrated with MGNREGA for sustainability. In-village infrastructure including tanks and pipelines expanded rapidly.

Year 2022:
Coverage reached 10.8 crore households marking 57% of target. A record 2.8 crore connections were added in single financial year. Goa, Telangana, and Dadra & Nagar Haveli achieved 100% coverage. Water quality monitoring through IMIS enabled remote tracking. Private sector participation through CSR and PPP models increased.

Year 2023:
Rural tap water coverage crossed 75% with 14.2 crore households connected. 12 states and UTs achieved 100% coverage status. Focus shifted to operation and maintenance sustainability. Community participation through Village Water & Sanitation Committees strengthened. Integration with Swachh Bharat and MGNREGA created synergies.

Year 2024:
Mission approached final targets with 16.5 crore households (87%) having tap connections. 20 states achieved 100% coverage creating pressure on lagging states. Water quality concerns addressed through 2 lakh testing centers. Election commitments accelerated implementation in remaining areas. Sustainability challenged by groundwater depletion in some regions.

Year 2025:
Near-universal coverage achieved with 18 crore households (95%) having functional tap connections. Mission focus shifted to long-term sustainability and service delivery quality. IoT sensors installed for real-time water quality and supply monitoring. Financial sustainability models developed for O&M. International recognition for world's largest rural water program.
