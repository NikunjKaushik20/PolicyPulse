Year 2015:
UDAY (Ujjwal DISCOM Assurance Yojana) was launched to revive financially stressed DISCOMs. States took over 75% of DISCOM debt on their books. Operational efficiency improvements mandatory. AT&C loss reduction targets set state-wise. Tariff rationalization and subsidy reform essential components.

Year 2018:
27 states and 5 UTs joined UDAY scheme. Rs 2.32 lakh crore state debt takeover completed. AT&C losses reduced from 21% to 18%. Many DISCOMs still struggling despite support. Revival linked to IPDS for infrastructure upgrade.

Year 2020:
COVID stressed DISCOM finances again. Liquidity infusion of Rs 90,000 crore provided. Power demand fluctuation challenged operations. Renewable energy integration increased complexity. Need for second round of restructuring emerged.

Year 2023:
Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme launched. UDAY lessons inform new approach. Smart metering mandatory for efficiency. Private sector participation in distribution grows. DISCOM health improves in several states.

Year 2025:
Distribution sector reforms showing results. Smart prepaid metering covering 50% consumers. AT&C losses approach 12% nationally. DISCOMs achieve near-breakeven in many states. Power sector viability significantly improved.
