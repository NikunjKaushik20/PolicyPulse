Year 2016:
Standup India was launched on April 5, 2016 to promote entrepreneurship among SC/ST and women. The scheme facilitated bank loans between Rs 10 lakh and Rs 1 crore for greenfield enterprises. Each bank branch required to extend at least one loan to SC/ST and one to women entrepreneur. Focus sectors included manufacturing, services, and trading. Credit guarantee provided through CGFSIL for loans up to Rs 50 lakh.

Year 2017:
First year saw 23,000 accounts opened with Rs 5,000 crore sanctioned. Women entrepreneurs received 80% of total loans. Handholding support through MUDRA and SIDBI strengthened. Success stories from rural women entrepreneurs promoted. Challenges included low awareness and complex documentation.

Year 2018:
Loan sanctions crossed Rs 10,000 crore milestone. SC/ST entrepreneur proportion improved with targeted outreach. Online portal launched for easier application tracking. Convergence with Skill India for beneficiary training. States with high SC/ST population showed stronger performance.

Year 2019:
Cumulative sanctions reached Rs 16,000 crore to 70,000 beneficiaries. Refinancing through SIDBI improved bank liquidity. Margin money reduced from 25% to 15% for easier access. Women-only entrepreneurship cells created in banks. Integration with PM SVANidhi for street vendor graduation planned.

Year 2020:
COVID impacted new sanctions but existing enterprises supported through ECLGS. Women entrepreneurs more affected due to sector concentration in services. Interest subvention provided for stressed accounts. Digital lending platforms integrated with scheme. Total sanctions declined to Rs 12,000 crore for the year.

Year 2021:
Recovery began with Rs 18,000 crore sanctioned. E-commerce and digital services new focus areas. Credit guarantee coverage enhanced to 85% of loan. Successful entrepreneurs mentored new applicants. SC entrepreneur proportion reached 25%.

Year 2022:
Scheme crossed 1 lakh cumulative beneficiaries. Manufacturing startups prioritized under Aatmanirbhar Bharat. District-level industrial meets for awareness. NPA rates remained below 5% showing scheme health. Women entrepreneur share steady at 78%.

Year 2023:
Revised guidelines simplified documentation requirements. Cluster-based approach for similar business support. Integration with GeM for market access. Annual sanctions touched Rs 22,000 crore. Target of 2.5 lakh beneficiaries by 2025 set.

Year 2024:
Election year focus increased scheme visibility. 1.5 lakh cumulative beneficiaries mark crossed. Green enterprise category added with additional support. NABARD extended scheme to agri-allied activities. Women entrepreneur success stories featured nationally.

Year 2025:
Scheme approaches 2 lakh beneficiary target. AI-based credit assessment reduced processing time. Export-oriented enterprises received priority. District-level incubation support expanded. Integration with ONDC for e-commerce access.
