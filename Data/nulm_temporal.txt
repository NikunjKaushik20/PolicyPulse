Year 2014:
Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) was launched for urban poor livelihoods. Focus on skill training, self-employment, and group enterprise. Social mobilization through Self Help Groups. Employment through City Livelihood Centers. Homeless shelters as safety net component.

Year 2018:
60 lakh urban poor mobilized into SHGs. 15 lakh trained and placed in jobs. 2000 shelters for urban homeless operational. Street vendor support integrated. Women enterprise development prioritized.

Year 2020:
COVID devastated urban poor livelihoods. Emergency food and support through NULM. Migrant worker return created challenges. Skill retraining for changed job market. Digital training became essential.

Year 2023:
1 crore urban poor in SHGs. Skill training aligned with Industry 4.0. Gig worker support mechanisms added. Housing for urban poor linked. Comprehensive urban poverty approach.

Year 2025:
Sustainable urban livelihoods for 1.5 crore families. Social security for urban informal workers. Housing-livelihood convergence achieved. Urban poverty reduction targets met. Cities become opportunity centers.
