Year 2016:
Startup India was launched on January 16, 2016 to promote entrepreneurship and innovation. The initiative offered tax exemptions, easier compliance, and funding support for recognized startups. Startup India Hub was created as single point of contact for ecosystem. Fund of Funds with Rs 10,000 crore corpus was announced to provide VC funding. Self-certification for labor and environmental laws reduced compliance burden.

Year 2017:
Recognition of startups crossed 5,000 with diverse sectors represented. Intellectual property facilitation enabled faster patent filings. International partnerships with Israel, Singapore, and US expanded opportunities. Women entrepreneur-focused programs were launched. Incubator network expanded to 300 across country.

Year 2018:
Inter-ministerial board simplified startup recognition process. Tax exemption extended to profits for 3 of first 7 years of operation. Angel tax issue emerged affecting early-stage funding. State startup rankings launched to encourage ecosystem development. Recognized startups crossed 15,000.

Year 2019:
Startup India crossed 25,000 recognized startups milestone. Angel tax concerns addressed with exemptions for registered startups. Fund of Funds investments reached Rs 3,500 crore committed. India became third largest startup ecosystem globally. Unicorn count reached 30+ demonstrating ecosystem maturity.

Year 2020:
COVID accelerated digital transformation creating opportunities despite challenges. Many startups pivoted to healthcare, edtech, and remote work solutions. Government launched Aatmanirbhar Bharat initiative supporting local startups. Startup India 2.0 focused on deep tech and manufacturing. Recognition crossed 40,000 startups.

Year 2021:
India added record 44 unicorns in single year showing ecosystem strength. Funding reached $42 billion, highest ever. Government enabled easier listing norms for startups on stock exchanges. Deep tech and fintech led investment categories. Startup recognition crossed 60,000.

Year 2022:
Funding winter began with global tech slowdown affecting valuations. Focus shifted to profitability over growth. Several startups faced layoffs and consolidation. Government incentivized semiconductor and EV startups. Recognition touched 85,000 despite challenges.

Year 2023:
Ecosystem stabilized with focus on sustainable business models. AI and climate tech emerged as priority sectors. Startup India crossed 1 lakh recognized startups. International expansion accelerated for mature startups. Women-led startups showed strongest growth.

Year 2024:
Recovery in funding with focus on profitable ventures. Election promises included enhanced support for MSME startups. Deep tech including space and defense opened to startups. District-level startup hubs launched. Recognition crossed 1.25 lakh.

Year 2025:
India positions as global startup hub with 1.5 lakh recognized startups. AI-first startups receive priority recognition and funding. Export-focused startups supported with market access programs. Gig economy workers from startups receive social security. Green startup certification launched for sustainable ventures.
