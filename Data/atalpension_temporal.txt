Year 2015:
Atal Pension Yojana was launched to provide pension security to workers in unorganized sector. The scheme guarantees monthly pension of Rs 1,000 to Rs 5,000 after age 60. Government contribution matched subscriber contribution up to Rs 1,000 annually for 5 years. Enrollment available for citizens aged 18-40 through bank accounts. Automatic debit from bank accounts ensured regular contribution.

Year 2016:
Enrollment crossed 50 lakh subscribers in first full year. Banks and post offices actively promoted scheme. Premium contribution linked to age and pension amount desired. Government co-contribution provided strong incentive for early enrollment. Focus on financial literacy to reach informal sector workers.

Year 2017:
Subscribers reached 80 lakh with increasing awareness. Digital enrollment through smartphones simplified onboarding. Scheme integrated with Jan Dhan accounts for seamless collection. Exit and withdrawal rules clarified for subscribers. Women enrollment reached 40% of total.

Year 2018:
APY crossed 1 crore subscribers milestone. Government ended co-contribution for new subscribers but continued for existing. Premium payment compliance challenges emerged. Scheme linked with PMJJBY and PMSBY for comprehensive coverage. Age of enrollment extended flexibility introduced.

Year 2019:
Enrollment reached 1.8 crore subscribers. Spouse joint account option introduced. Digital certificates and statements provided through DigiLocker. Premium payment reminders through SMS improved compliance. Scheme promoted through CSC and banking correspondents.

Year 2020:
COVID created awareness for retirement planning. Enrollment accelerated to 2.3 crore despite pandemic. Online enrollment and payment options expanded. Government clarified that APY continues for those joining PM schemes. Premium payment defaults addressed through flexible options.

Year 2021:
Subscribers crossed 3 crore showing strong momentum. Exit before 60 rules simplified for genuine cases. Integration with e-SHRAM database initiated. Pension amount options above Rs 5,000 explored. Premium auto-debit compliance improved.

Year 2022:
APY reached 4 crore subscribers. Taxpayers above threshold excluded from enrollment. Scheme sustainability analysis showed healthy corpus. Digital nomination and modification enabled. Women subscribers crossed 45%.

Year 2023:
Enrollment touched 5 crore subscribers. Flexi-contribution option allowing variable payments introduced. Aadhaar-based enrollment simplified process. Scheme corpus crossed Rs 20,000 crore. Return on investment exceeded projections.

Year 2024:
APY neared 6 crore subscribers. Pension amount options enhanced with higher slabs. Integration with EPFO for organized sector portability explored. Spouse continuation benefits clarified. Marketing through gig platforms increased.

Year 2025:
Scheme approaches 7 crore subscribers. AI-based retirement planning tools offered to subscribers. Inflation-indexed pension option explored. Universal pension integration with old-age benefits considered. Corpus crossed Rs 40,000 crore ensuring sustainability.
