Year 1998:
Kisan Credit Card scheme was launched to provide affordable credit to farmers for agricultural and allied activities. The scheme offered revolving credit facility with flexible withdrawal and repayment. Initial interest rates were at 12% with subvention bringing effective rate lower. Coverage included crop loans, working capital, and investment credit. Insurance coverage for KCC holders was introduced.

Year 2005:
KCC coverage expanded to include animal husbandry and fisheries activities. Cards issued crossed 5 crore with outstanding credit of Rs 1 lakh crore. Interest subvention of 2% introduced for timely repayment. Personal accident insurance of Rs 50,000 integrated. Simplified single-window application through banks.

Year 2010:
KCC crossed 10 crore active cards nationally. RuPay-enabled cards replaced paper-based system. ATM withdrawal facility added for greater flexibility. Credit limit calculation based on landholding and crop pattern. Integration with core banking improved tracking.

Year 2015:
Comprehensive restructuring with expanded scope. Interest subvention increased to 3% for prompt repayment. Effective interest rate of 4% for timely payers. Card validity extended to 5 years. Insurance coverage enhanced to Rs 2 lakh.

Year 2018:
PM-KISAN beneficiaries targeted for KCC saturation. Campaign to provide KCC to all PM-KISAN farmers. Digital Aadhaar-based issuance initiated. 15 crore active cards in the system. Outstanding credit crossed Rs 6 lakh crore.

Year 2019:
Extended to fisheries and animal husbandry as separate scheme. 2 crore additional farmers brought into KCC fold. App-based balance and limit checks introduced. Interest subvention maintained at 3%. Online renewal simplified process.

Year 2020:
COVID disruptions led to payment moratorium for KCC loans. Special concessional credit for distressed farmers. 18 crore cards with Rs 8 lakh crore outstanding. Emergency working capital for Kharif season. Digital KCC issuance accelerated.

Year 2021:
Focus on saturation of all PM-KISAN beneficiaries with KCC. 5 crore new cards issued in saturation drive. Dairy and fisheries KCC crossed 50 lakh. Interest subvention budget Rs 20,000 crore. Mobile-first KCC application.

Year 2022:
Credit flow to agriculture sector Rs 15 lakh crore target. KCC contributed major share of farm credit. Allied activities including apiculture added. NPAs in KCC remained below 5%. Digital credit assessment using land records.

Year 2023:
20 crore active KCC cards achieved. Climate-resilient crop insurance integration. Precision agriculture credit products explored. Interest rates competitive with market conditions. Comprehensive farmer credit score developed.

Year 2024:
Election year KCC saturation drives intensified. Small and marginal farmer focus. Allied sector credit expanded significantly. App-based disbursement reduced paperwork. Agricultural credit portal integration.

Year 2025:
AI-based credit assessment using satellite crop data. Interest subvention system modernized through DBT. KCC for tenant farmers streamlined with state records. Green credit for sustainable farming practices. National farmer credit registry developed.
