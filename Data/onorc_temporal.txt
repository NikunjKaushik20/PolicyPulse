Year 2019:
One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) was launched to enable portability of food security benefits. Migrant workers could access rations anywhere in India through Aadhaar authentication. Pilot started in 4 states with biometric verified transactions. Linked to NFSA entitlements for subsidized food grains. Target to cover all states and UTs by March 2021.

Year 2020:
COVID accelerated ONORC urgency for stranded migrants. 17 states integrated by August 2020. 11 crore portability transactions recorded. Free food grains announced under PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana. Aadhaar seeding of ration cards expedited.

Year 2021:
All 36 States and UTs integrated on ONORC platform. 80 crore beneficiaries covered under national portability. Inter-state transactions crossed 50 crore cumulative. Free ration scheme extended under PMGKAY. Migrant worker registration improved.

Year 2022:
100 crore portability transactions milestone. ONORC cited as world's largest food distribution network. State-wise portability rates varied significantly. Intra-state portability helped within-state migrants. ration card portability linked to voter ID reforms discussions.

Year 2023:
PMGKAY integrated with regular NFSA. 150 crore cumulative transactions under ONORC. Digital ration cards via Mera Ration app. Real-time verification reduced fraudulent claims. Distribution efficiency improved 20%.

Year 2024:
Election year continued free food grain scheme. 200 crore transactions under ONORC. Facial authentication piloted alongside Aadhaar. Grievance redressal improved. Near-universal ration card portability achieved.

Year 2025:
ONORC transforms food security delivery. 250 crore cumulative transactions. AI-based fraud detection operational. Migrant worker food security normalized. India model studied by other countries.
