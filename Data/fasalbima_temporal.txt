Year 2016:
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana was launched to provide comprehensive crop insurance at low premium rates. The scheme replaced multiple older schemes with uniform implementation across states. Premium rates were fixed at 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi crops and 5% for horticulture. Sum insured was based on Scale of Finance ensuring adequate coverage. Technology integration through satellite imagery and drones was initiated for loss assessment.

Year 2017:
Scheme covered 5.7 crore farmer applications across 27 states. Claims paid reached Rs 16,000 crore demonstrating financial protection during crop failures. Sum insured crossed Rs 2 lakh crore marking massive coverage scale. Smartphone-based crop cutting experiments began reducing assessment time. Area discrepancy concerns emerged between insured and actual cultivated land.

Year 2018:
Enrollment remained high with 5.8 crore applications. Government decided to make scheme voluntary instead of mandatory for loanee farmers. Restructured guidelines focused on improving farmer awareness and claim settlement. Insurance companies faced losses in many states due to adverse weather. State subsidy arrears accumulated creating payment delays.

Year 2019:
Scheme saw decline with 4.4 crore farmer applications. Several states withdrew citing unaffordable subsidy burden. Private sector insurance companies exited unprofitable regions. Claim settlement delays of 6-8 months frustrated enrolled farmers. Technology solutions including PMFBY portal improved transparency.

Year 2020:
COVID disruptions affected enrollment and crop cutting experiments. Locust attacks and unseasonal rain benefited insured farmers. Government restructured scheme with enhanced technology integration. Insurance companies mandated to create district-level grievance mechanisms. Total applications dropped to 3.9 crore.

Year 2021:
Scheme revamped with state-wise caps on premium subsidies. Several states resumed participation with improved terms. Claim processing time reduced to 2 months through technology. Beed model of crop insurance with fixed per-acre premium piloted. Farmer awareness campaigns intensified.

Year 2022:
Weather-based crop insurance component expanded. Satellite and AI-based remote sensing reduced field visits. Fasal Rahat scheme provided alternative in some states. Premium collection and claims stabilized. Coverage improved with 4.5 crore applications.

Year 2023:
Scheme completed 7 years with Rs 1.5 lakh crore cumulative claims paid. NABARD enabled easier claim processing for cooperative banks. Integration with PM-KISAN ensured premium deduction coordination. Horticulture coverage expanded significantly. Farmer complaints about inadequate assessment reduced.

Year 2024:
Election year focus on farmer welfare increased enrollment focus. Premium subsidy limits reviewed upward for small farmers. States encouraged to reduce premium burden on beneficiaries. All states and UTs brought under scheme coverage. Digital claim settlement within 30 days piloted.

Year 2025:
AI-powered yield estimation using satellite data became standard. Parametric insurance based on weather indices expanded. Blockchain for transparent premium and claim tracking piloted. Coverage expanded to allied activities including fisheries and animal husbandry. Integration with agricultural credit systems streamlined.
