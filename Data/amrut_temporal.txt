Year 2015:
Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) was launched to provide basic services in 500 cities. Focus on water supply, sewerage, urban transport, and green spaces. Total investment of Rs 50,000 crore planned over 5 years. States given flexibility in project prioritization. Reform-linked funding to improve urban governance.

Year 2016:
500 cities covered under first phase. State Annual Action Plans approved worth Rs 77,000 crore. Water supply capacity addition of 5 MLD. Sewerage network projects initiated. Municipal finance reforms mandated.

Year 2017:
Reform implementation accelerated. Credit ratings for cities introduced. Water supply projects covered 45 lakh households. Urban transport including metros and buses prioritized. Green spaces development initiated.

Year 2018:
Mid-term review showed significant progress. 139 lakh households benefited from water supply. Sewerage network coverage reached 30% of target. Municipal bond issuance by cities encouraged. Park and playground development crossed 1,000.

Year 2019:
AMRUT 1.0 targets substantially achieved. Water supply to 2 crore households. Sewerage coverage to 1 crore households. City finance reforms showing results. Urban flooding mitigation incorporated.

Year 2020:
COVID highlighted urban infrastructure needs. Clean water access critical for hygiene. Handwashing stations installed under AMRUT. Project timelines extended due to pandemic. Emphasis on sustainable water management.

Year 2021:
AMRUT 2.0 launched for all urban areas. Extended coverage to all 4,387 statutory towns. Focus on making cities water secure. Universal water supply coverage target set. Rejuvenation of water bodies included.

Year 2022:
3 crore tap connections under AMRUT 2.0 target. Sewerage in 500 AMRUT cities focus. Grey and black water recycling promoted. Climate proofing urban infrastructure. Smart water meters piloted.

Year 2023:
50% of AMRUT 2.0 physical targets achieved. Water recycling reached 20% in major cities. Municipal revenue improvement continued. Digital infrastructure mapping completed. Urban flooding solutions implemented.

Year 2024:
AMRUT 2.0 moving towards completion. Universal urban water supply approaching. Sewerage gap reducing significantly. Green and blue infrastructure expanded. Municipal creditworthiness improved.

Year 2025:
Near-universal urban water supply. Circular economy in water management. Climate-resilient urban infrastructure standard. All cities with functional sewerage. Smart urban water networks operational.
