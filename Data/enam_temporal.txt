Year 2016:
Electronic National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) was launched to create unified national market for agricultural commodities. Online trading platform connecting APMC mandis across states. Price discovery, transparency, and better farmer realization as goals. Initial integration of 21 mandis across 8 states. Inter-state trade facilitation as key objective.

Year 2018:
585 mandis integrated on e-NAM platform. 1.66 crore farmers and 1.31 lakh traders registered. Assaying infrastructure at mandis expanded. Logistics integration for inter-mandi trade. States reformed APMC acts for e-NAM compatibility.

Year 2020:
COVID highlighted importance of digital agriculture markets. Contactless trading enabled. FPO integration for collective marketing. 1000 mandis brought online. Direct farmer-buyer linkages strengthened.

Year 2023:
1361 mandis integrated. 3 crore farmers registered. Warehouse-based trading added. Quality testing labs at all mandis. Real-time price information on mobile. Integration with PM-KISAN beneficiary data.

Year 2025:
Near-universal APMC integration. Unified national agricultural market realized. Farmer price realization improved 15%. Export integration for quality produce. AI-based price forecasting helping farmers.
