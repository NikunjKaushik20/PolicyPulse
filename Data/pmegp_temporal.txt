Year 2008:
Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) was launched merging PMRY and REGP schemes. Focus on generating continuous and sustainable self-employment in rural and urban areas. Maximum project cost Rs 25 lakh for manufacturing and Rs 10 lakh for service sector. Subsidy of 15-35% depending on location and category. KVIC and state KVIB as implementing agencies.

Year 2015:
Scheme crossed 50 lakh beneficiaries cumulative. Average loan size increased with project expansion. Manufacturing units showed higher survival rates. Integration with Skill India for trained entrepreneurs. District-level targets set for focused implementation.

Year 2020:
COVID impacted new enterprise creation. Emergency support for existing PMEGP units under ECLGS. Digital application process accelerated. Micro-units prioritized for quick employment generation. 8 lakh units created during pandemic year.

Year 2023:
Scheme covered 75 lakh cumulative beneficiaries. Subsidy rates enhanced for women and SC/ST. E-commerce integration for market access. Quality certification support added. Green manufacturing incentives introduced.

Year 2025:
1 crore cumulative beneficiaries target approached. AI-based business viability assessment. Export-oriented units receive priority. Circular economy manufacturing promoted. PMEGP becomes gateway to formal MSME status.
